Empowering Youth: Kalam often encouraged young people to be unique in a world trying to make them like everyone else, urging them to become leaders in their fields.
Overcoming Failure: In his famous 1979 SLV-3 failure anecdote, he taught that failures are "First Attempts In Learning" (FAIL) and that one must persevere to succeed.
Vision for India: He emphasized making India a strong, self-reliant, developed nation through technology, agriculture, and education.
Character and Education: He believed that education should foster creativity, righteousness in the heart, and character, which leads to harmony in the home and society.
